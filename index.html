About Me

My name is Nick, and I’m an Economics & Business student at the University of Waterloo. I’m curious by nature and driven by learning. I love playing the guitar, Indonesian food, and anything related to finance. I also enjoy getting lost in fiction, where my imagination has room to wander. That love of learning is what inspired me to build this site. I’m a firm believer in lifelong growth.

Outside of academics, I’m a varsity athlete on the university’s Track and Field team, specializing in long jump (current PB: 6.74 m). I play a mix of sports, including volleyball, basketball, and soccer. I enjoy staying active, and I hope that I can keep on playing sports throughout my lifetime.

I also run the UW Public Speaking Club, where I get to share my love of storytelling with the UW community. We host weekly workshops built around games, discussion, and real practice. It’s less like a classroom and more like a community. You can check out what we’re up to on our Instagram.

If you’re curious about what I’m working on, feel free to contact me!

Experience

Take a look at some roles that have shaped who I am.

UW Public Speaking Club

August 2025 - Present

I took over this club, with the hopes of putting my leadership skills to the test. I built the framework for the club, and got to work with 9 other students. Running a club has taught me the importance of effective leadership. There is so much to learn, and I am in the process of discovering what style I lead with.

Varsity Track and Field

October 2023 - Present

Funny enough, I actually started at UW playing varsity football. I later transitioned over to track and field because I wanted to learn how to bet on myself. Varsity taught me the importance of discipline and hard work. I learned that progress isn't made in the spotlight, but rather when nobody is watching. Those days where I didn't feel like going to train, yet still showed up, helped shaped my character.

Definity Financial Corporation

September 2025 - December 2025

One of my first co-ops was at Definity. Here, I learned how to work within systems. I found ways to optimize existing workflows, and put my own personal touch on these improvements. I leveraged AI to speed our work up, revamped the KPI file, and created a "Co-Op Bible" that served as a guide for future co-ops. I also learned so much from those around me. It goes to show that there is so much to learn from others, and many stories to be heard.

HomeView Window Cleaning

April 2024 - August 2025

Me and my friend founded HomeView back in the summer of '24. At first, we had everything to learn. Knocking on strangers' doors for the first time trying to sell something was like telling a 5-year old to do a double backflip. It was terrifying at first, but we eventually became more confident. I learned how to pitch effectively and pursuasively during my time running the business. I worked with a small team, and taught sales pitches on the frontend, and managed the cash on the backend. It was my first stint at running my own business.
